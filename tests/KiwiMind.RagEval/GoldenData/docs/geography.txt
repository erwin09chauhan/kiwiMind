Mount Everest is the highest mountain above sea level on Earth, standing at 8,849 meters at its peak in the Himalayas.

The Amazon River carries more water than any other river in the world and flows through Brazil, Peru, and several other South American countries.

The Sahara is the largest hot desert on Earth, covering much of North Africa with an area of about 9.2 million square kilometers.

Lake Baikal in Siberia is the deepest and oldest freshwater lake in the world, holding roughly 20 percent of the planet's unfrozen fresh water.

The Mariana Trench in the western Pacific Ocean is the deepest oceanic trench on Earth, reaching a depth of nearly 11,000 meters.
