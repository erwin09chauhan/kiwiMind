The giant sequoia is one of the tallest and most massive tree species on Earth, with some individuals living for over 3,000 years.

Bamboo is technically a grass and is among the fastest-growing plants in the world, with some species growing nearly a meter in a single day.

Cacti store water in their thick stems to survive in arid desert environments, and their spines help reduce water loss and deter predators.

The Venus flytrap is a carnivorous plant native to subtropical wetlands that snaps shut to trap insects when tiny hairs on its leaves are triggered.

Sunflowers exhibit heliotropism, meaning young flower heads track the sun's movement across the sky from east to west during the day.
